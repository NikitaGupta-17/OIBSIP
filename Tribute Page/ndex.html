Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

" Missile Man of India , The People's President "


Time-line of his Life..

Avul Plair Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam widely known as India’s Missile Man and was India’s 11th President as well as an aerospace scientist. For five years, he served as India’s President (2002-2007) He was instrumental in the development of India’s nuclear and missile programmes.

  • 1931 - Born on 15th October in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College,Tiruchirappali.
  • 1955 - Enrolled at the MIT to study aerospace engineering.
  • 1960 - Joined DRDO as a scientist.
  • 1965 - Started work rocket project independently at DRDO.
  • 1969 - Joined Space Research at ISRO.
  • 1980 - Became the project Director for India's First Indegenous Satellite Program.
  • 1980 - Was involved in the development of several Indegenous Missiles for India like Agni, Prithvi.
  • 1981 - Awarded Padma Bhushan.
  • 1990 - Awarded Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1992-1999 - Chief Scientific Advisor to the PM and the Secretary of DRDO.
  • 1997 - Awarded Bharat Ratna.
  • 2002 - Became 11th President of India.
  • 2012 - Launch mission for the youth of the nation called "What Can I Give Movement".
  • 2015 - The People's President passes away while doing what he loved the most, addressing students in Shillong, Meghalaya.

"If you want to shine like sun, first burn like sun."
